Introduction

In this analysis we explore some trends that female labour force participation (LFP) has followed since 1980 in OECD countries.
After finding that WLFP has grown at very different rates, and up to various levels, in different regions of the world –being
particularly high in Nordic countries– we use visualization tools to dig deeper into some potential partial explanations for this phenomenon.

For example, public policies incentivizing the substitution of leisure or household work with market labor
might partially explain higher this country-level differences.

Choose a variable to explore how it correlates with female LFP (remember: this is merely exploratory!).
